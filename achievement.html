Researches

Production of Antibiotic Resistant Bacteria Found in Soil

Jul 2, 2019 - University of Minnesota and Minneapolis college

Antibiotic resistance has become one of the largest health threats to the public's health . Bacteria is becoming increasingly resistant to the antibiotics that are currently available. In this project our main goal was finding bacteria that can produce novel antibiotics. First step was collecting the soil. then we conducted serial dilution to decrease the microbes in the soil, after that we started isolate colonies of bacteria from each other and purified them using Streak plate method. After we got a single colony of bacteria, we screened for isolate antibiotic growth via Spread/Patch Technique( as shown in figure 2) and Patch/Patch Technique(figure 3). For Spread/Patch Technique we got positive result, however we did not get any positive result from Patch/Patch Technique . After that, these strains were classified strains morphologically and characterized for gram staining. The strains that showed production of antibiotics in Spread/Patch Technique were test again using Biochemical test such as Mannitol Salt Agar ( For positive –gram bacteria) and MacConkey agar(For Negative – gram bacteria). These strain were than tested as resistant to ampicillin ,penicillin and other antimicrobial agents using Disk Diffusion Test(Figure 4).Finally, we conducted Polymerase Chain Reaction(PCR) using Gel electrophoresis technique(Figure 5) to amplify a segment of 16S rRNA gene sequence.16S rRNA gene is used to determine the kind of bacteria. However we did not get any result due to trouble shooting.
